The GTM Interview & Visualization Playbook

A Repeatable Framework for Turning Expert Interviews into High-Impact GTM Assets

Why This Playbook?

This playbook provides a simple, standardized module of questions and corresponding visualization templates. By incorporating these questions into your expert interviews for each micro-community, you provide the GTM team with the exact fuel needed to create compelling, data-driven assets. This collaboration turns your invaluable qualitative work into a predictable engine for generating headline statistics, insightful frameworks, and market maps that drive qualified meetings with our target clients.

Theme 1: Measuring Trends

Goal: To capture the direction and magnitude of market shifts (e.g., spending, adoption).

Question Template: "Looking at your budget for [Sub_Vertical], is your spend increasing, decreasing, or flat year-over-year? By what rough percentage range? (decreasing, same, Increase: ≤5%, 5–15%, 15–30%, >30%)"

Visualization: Horizontal Bar Chart

Best for: Quickly showing the dominant trend. Highly readable and impactful for any number of respondents.

Google Sheets Guide: This is a standard "Bar chart". Input your categories in one column and the count of responses in the next.

Theme 2: Measuring Allocation

Goal: To understand resources trends, creates interesting visual elements.

Question Template: "Thinking about that increase in [Topic] spend, could you distribute 100 points among these three categories based on where the money is going: Tooling/Software, Headcount, and Managed Services?"

Visualization A: 100% Stacked Bar Chart

Best for: A clean, powerful visual showing the proportional breakdown of a whole. Excellent for showing budget allocation.

Google Sheets Guide: Use the "100% stacked bar chart" type. Calculate the average points for each category from your interview data and input them as separate series.

Visualization B: Waffle Chart (for Web/PDF)

Best for: I think these charts work really well for final reports or social media assets. Not available out of the box with Canva/GSheets but not too difficult to build manually.

Theme 3: Measuring Sentiment, Priorities & Headlines

Goal: To uncover potential provocative headlines, quantify qualitative feelings, and rank what matters most.

Q1 (Sentiment): "On a scale of 1-5, how prepared is your organization to manage the risks from [Threat]?"

Q2 (Priorities): "What is your #1 investment priority for [Topic] from this list: [Option A], [Option B], or [Option C]?" (if needed, add ? "Other—specify"?)

Q3 (Headline): "In the world of [Sub_Vertical], what is the single most overrated trend right now, and what is the most underrated one?"

Theme 5: Eliciting Frameworks

Goal: To synthesize expert mental models into an actionable diligence framework.

Question Template: "Imagine you're advising someone looking to acquire a company in the [Sub_Vertical] space. Beyond the standard financial diligence, what are the top 2-3 non-obvious red flags you would tell them to look for?"

Visualization: The 1-Page Diligence Framework

Best for: Creating the "Hero Asset" for a GTM campaign. This synthesizes qualitative answers into a high-value, actionable checklist for our PE audience.

3 Critical Tests for Cybersecurity Diligence

Synthesized from interviews with 15 leading CISOs

Test #1: The AI Governance Test

The Red Flag: Don’t ask “How are you using AI?”. Our experts tell us the critical question is “How are you governing AI?”. This test helps you uncover hidden liabilities before they impact valuation.
The Killer Question: "Beyond a simple Acceptable Use Policy, what technical controls are in place to prevent proprietary code or sensitive data from being used to train public AI models?"

Test #2: The Vendor Value Test

The Red Flag: Challenge the assumption that more tools = more security. Our experts report that vendor sprawl creates operational chaos and a significant drain on resources.
The Killer Question: "Walk us through your process for decommissioning a redundant security tool. How many have you decommissioned in the last 18 months?"

Test #3: The Financial Risk Test

The Red Flag: A target company cannot translate cybersecurity risks into financial terms. This indicates a lack of maturity and could hide significant future costs for remediation and compliance.
The Killer Question: "Can you provide the risk register that quantifies the potential financial impact of your top three identified cybersecurity threats?"
